Highlights of the City

1. Chhota Imambara Lucknow

Chota Imambara, also known as Hussainabad Imambara, is a beautiful monument in Lucknow, built in 1838 by Nawab Muhammad Ali Shah. It's renowned for its intricate architecture, chandeliers, and decorations. The structure serves as a mausoleum and is a significant symbol of Lucknow's heritage.

2. Roomi Darwaza

Rumi Darwaza, built in 1784 by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula in Lucknow, is an iconic example of Awadhi architecture. Standing 60 feet tall. This majestic structure showcases intricate designs and serves as a symbol of the city's historical grandeur. It was constructed as part of a relief project during a famine, providing work and sustenance to thousands of people.

3. Bara Imambara

Bara Imambara, built in 1784 by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula in Lucknow, is a grand architectural marvel. Known for its central hall, one of the largest arched constructions without support beams, it also features a labyrinthine maze called Bhool Bhulaiya. This historical monument symbolizes Lucknow's rich Mughal heritage and architectural prowess.

Best Food of the City

1. Lucknawi Chicken Biryani

Lucknawi Biryani, a fragrant and flavorful rice dish, hails from Lucknow and is a hallmark of Awadhi cuisine. Known for its delicate aroma and rich taste, it combines basmati rice with marinated meat, saffron, and a blend of spices. Slow-cooked in a sealed pot, this biryani epitomizes the royal culinary heritage of Lucknow.


2. Tunday-Kababi

Tunday Kebabi, a culinary gem from Lucknow, is famed for its melt-in-the-mouth texture and rich flavor. Created by the one-armed chef Haji Murad Ali ("Tunday"), these kebabs combine finely minced meat with a secret blend of spices. Often served with parathas or roomali rotis, they are a must-try dish that epitomizes Lucknow's royal cuisine.


1. Lucknawi Kulfi

Lucknawi Kulfi is a traditional Indian ice cream known for its rich, creamy texture and exquisite flavors. Made with thickened milk, sugar, and a mix of saffron, cardamom, and nuts, it's slow-cooked and then frozen in molds. This delightful dessert is a quintessential part of Lucknow's culinary heritage, offering a perfect, refreshing treat.
